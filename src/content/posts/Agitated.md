Norman was getting ready for work. He looked the weather up on his phone: light snowfall at -4 degrees. He’d known it would be cold. Thick socks were already on, his warm pants and sweater as well. He put on his winter-jacket and gloves. Now he was looking for his new beanie which he had ordered online three weeks ago. It had been lying on the little table in the corridor- the one where there a long mirror hung above it. 

He went to reach for it… but then he hesitated. Lisa didn’t like it. She’s always wanted him to wear her beanie, meaning the one she had bought for him. The one that was out of some wool-like material that ensured a perfectly scratchy head. Her beanie which was porous with many awry fibres that made it look as opposite of neat as it could. The beanie which didn’t keep warm at all because of its porous texture- the wind blew through it like nothing. This beanie was on one of the three little wooden handles atop the mirror.

Fuck it, he thought. I’d rather have a cold, scratchy head- and look funky on the side, than have another fight-discussion with Lisa. So he straightened up his back and made to reach for her beanie- and in doing so he caught a glimpse of himself in the mirror. For the first time in forever, he was startled from what he saw and drew his hand back. “What was up with my eyes?” He thought. “Eyes of a… rabbit? This imagery cropped up in his mind. Was what he saw harmlessness? No. Children are harmless, yet they don’t have such a look in their eyes. Their eyes can’t radiate intimidation, but they do radiate hopefulness, curiosity, adventure. Mine reflected the opposite. 

Docility? A horse is docile, so is a cow or a dog. Their eyes met mine with an openness whenever I got the chance to be in their presence. Openness, and calm interest- in search of some occasional treat. Expectant in their attitude but not fearsome or subjugated. They hadn’t had these attributes that my eyes showed right now.”

“So my eyes had something like fear in them?” He thought to himself ”-but why?

“Did i fear going against the wishes of Lisa? No, It’s completely natural to want to do something that would make one’s other half happy. “ he tried to assure himself, but something inside him whispered what if it wasn’t? Would that make her happy anyway? You know how she is. 

“She sure does have a propensity to be annoyed by many things- even unhappy, one might say” he thought.

“But do I have to make her happy at any cost? Do I have to sacrifice my own desires for her to be happy? How does her happiness come with such an extensive list of terms and conditions- so as for me to restrict myself from wearing one damned beanie. “ He furrowed his brows and his eyes shine with fury. His lips had wrinkled from the tight shut he gave them.

“If it at least were reciprocal! When I am the one to advise her what to wear or not to wear- she does this bitchy face and tells me she clothes herself for HER-SELF. Yet in my case I should also bow and bend and disfigure my needs to fit hers. That is the curse of a utterly selfish partner” he thought and grabbed his beanie. The one he had chosen. The one HE had desired. And wore it tightly over his ears. Then he remembered what he saw in the mirror. The glimpse of himself was the essence of cowardice. Of a wormy, non-vertebrate being, far- far worse than a fucking rabbit. A virtue reserved for the people who are always being pulled by a metaphorical leash to do the bid of others. An existence to never cause any turbulence. An existence that never makes itself known- terrified of the notion of troubled waters. 

“I’ll show her” he thought. He had planned to leave without being seen by her. But now he wanted to show his token of protest. It is but a symbolic gesture to signify the chains that have been broken by his iron will to stop being an untermensch. From now on he would be not led, but he would lead.

He went into the kitchen to take an Apple from the fridge. An apple he ironically did not want. She was doing her make-up with the help of a legged mirror on the table. She had noticed. “Norman!” She said. 

“Yes?” He returned. He was ready for it. 

“Take this beanie off, the other one is above the mirror in the corridor.” She said curtly to show annoyance.

“No.” He said. His blood running wild in his body. His hot head had his ears color into a ridiculous dark pink underneath his beanie.

“No?” She said raising her eyebrow in surprise.

Didn’t expect it did you? Try me, now. Try me!

“Why not?” She said.

“Because I like this one.” He said. Come on now, do your thing… start something.

She dropped one of her make-up thingies into the make-up bag.

Here it comes!

She took another thingy out of her makeup bag and started on her lips. As a by-the-by she said. “Oh alrighty then sweetie, have a nice day!”

“Uh… okay. You too.” 

He left for work.
