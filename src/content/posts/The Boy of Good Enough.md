title: "The Boy of Good Enough"

In a house on a hill lived a boy, don’t you know,
Who did things sort-of-kind-of wherever he’d go.
His name was young Ollie, and here was his way:
He’d finish things mostly, then call it a day!

When Ollie swept leaves, he’d miss quite a few,
When Ollie tied shoes, he’d tie one, not two.
When Ollie made pancakes, he’d flip them half-round,
Then smile a big smile and make this sound:

“Good enough! Good enough! That’s fine by me!
Good enough! Good enough! As good as can be!”
He’d brush most his teeth (but not all thirty-two),
He’d wash most his dishes (but skip one or two).

He’d color inside lines (well, mostly he’d try),
Then grin his big grin as he’d give out this cry:
“Good enough! Good enough! That’s fine by me!
Good enough! Good enough! As good as can be!”

But Mother looked worried when sheets weren’t tucked tight,
And Sister felt sad when her bike wasn’t right.
And Father felt puzzled when tools weren’t put back,
And Grandma felt bothered by Ollie’s half-snack.

“Oh my!” said his Mother, “Oh dear!” said his Pop,
“Oh golly!” said Sister, “This has got to stop!”
But Ollie just wondered, “Why can’t they just see?
Good enough should be good enough for me!”
  
He thought they were fussy, he thought they were mean,
The pickiest, fussiest folks he had seen!
But then something happened that made Ollie think,
As he sat by himself with some milk he couldn’t drink.

(The milk had gone sour - he’d left it all day,
Because putting it back was too much delay.)
So Ollie decided to try something new,
To see what his very-best-trying could do.

He swept every leaf from the very first one,
He tied both his shoes when his sweeping was done.
He flipped every pancake till golden and round,
And listened for this most remarkable sound:

“Wonderful!” Mother said, clapping with glee,
“Amazing!” said Father, “How happy are we!”
“Fantastic!” said Sister, “You tried extra hard!”
“Stupendous!” said Grandma out in the yard.
  
Now Ollie discovered a secret so bright:
When you try extra-hard, it makes things feel right!
It’s not about perfect, it’s not about best,
It’s caring enough to give more than the rest.

So now when young Ollie has work to be done,
He thinks of the smiles when his trying is fun!
And though he’s not perfect (and that’s quite all right),
He tries a bit harder to make their day bright!
