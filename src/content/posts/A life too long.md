The man clipped his nose hairs in front of the bathroom mirror. He usually doesn't mind them, nor does he notice them becoming too long- But the quiet voice of his wife advised him to clip them in the morning as he was reading the newspaper. He had folded the paper neatly and had placed it beside his half-drank coffee. What is left of it, he would be enjoying cold. He usually would protest against such a wish of his wife when he is enjoying his preferred form of mental stimulation, but recently that had changed. 
He did his best with the hairs, but had to redo some parts of it after examining himself with his glasses. They didn’t protrude disgustingly from his nostrils anymore. His eyes wandered upwards to the bald top of his head, which reflected the harsh ceiling light. This bald surface was encircled on three sides with the remaining white and grey hair and frontally with his thinning eyebrows. His blue eyes were droopy and disinterested. Eyebags sagged heavily and the lower eyelids were watery. He gave off a sorry impression and he didn’t enjoy it. His thin lips curved in distaste under a patch of wiry mustache.
He had enough of his reflection, switched off the light and went to his table. He started reading his newspaper again and sipped on his cold coffee. Advising, the wife did gladly- but she didn't seem to want to compliment him for being so well-kept. He said nothing. He did wait patiently though, on some off-chance of a positive commentary. He guessed that he will have to get used to the silence and absence of motivating statements from his beloved wife. It‘s alright, he thought. Sometime even that had to fizzle out.
The world had only bad news to offer; Famine, corruption, terrorist attacks and staggering failures of the ministry of defense. Department of education didn‘t seem any better neither. *When things want to go bad, everything goes to hell at once*, he thought.
*Does it get better?* He asked himself. *How long has it been now?* 
His wife knew and interjected. ‘’In two days it will be three weeks.’’
He nodded. Soon-to-be three weeks since he had buried her. 
*‘til now it’s  only gotten worse.*

---

The man lay awake in his bed for longer than usual. He could not sleep well. He had rented the apartment above his to a late-twenties foreign couple, which enjoyed being a little noisy in the late evenings. *Ahh… When I was young… my energy was just as inexhaustable.*  The old man thought. *I bedded my wife just like the young lad does his. I used to make this whole building shake.* 
He’d thought that hearing the neighbors late-night shag would be as far as it’d go for inconvenience. Alas, he was terribly mistaken. The noise that permeated his evenings was a low-pitched rumbling of an air-compressor in combination with the high-pitched whirring of a turbine. They were dentists that did exercises in their apartment outside of work. He would prefer they did it instead of this.
*God bless ‘em.* He massaged his temples. 
Sometime he’d meet them on the floor as they went up the stairs. He’d stammer when he talked to the dentists and tried his best to create a friendlier- a more familial relation. His wife had wanted that before she passed on. ‘’Go drink a few beers with the young lad!’’ She said when her disease left her without energy. This was of course a desire that would satisfy her two-fold: Her husband would weld a friendship he would need after she’d depart and she’d get an hour of crumbling in bed into a pitiful state- far out of reach of her beloved husband’s vision. He couldn‘t be convinced to leave her side in her last months.
At a later point, when an automated window at this couple’s apartment that opens and closes at a push of a button malfuncted- he tried to close in on the two of them by trying to be relatable and fun about it.
*Don’t worry!* He had said. *We will get to the **root** of this issue!* He laughed. 
He didn’t want a friendship with them. That much was clear to him now. He had ruminated about why he kept finding excuses when he was invited in for some tea. 
*All the company I ever needed was Francine.*
