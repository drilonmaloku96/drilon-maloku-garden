
My dad takes the trains in a curious way,
He asks every minute, “Which one’s today?”
When we’re on the train, he still needs to know,
“Which platform next? Where do we go?”

He checks on his phone, he squints and he sighs,
He doubts every answer, no matter the size.
But once he relaxes, the questions subside—
He opens a book and lets time slide.  

Then softly he whispers, each word in a creak,
That somehow can travel from seat to seat.
It’s quiet yet piercing, a whispering crow,
And everyone hears though he doesn’t know.

When dinnertime comes, oh, what a sound!
A slurp, a breath, and a gulp all around!
He chews through his mouth, he hums through his nose,
And sauce somehow finds its way to his clothes.

When talking, he shouts like a thunderous bell,
For his hearing is going (though he won’t tell).
And if you should say, “Please stop that, Dad,”
He’ll double it down, just to drive you mad!

He’ll tease and he’ll poke with a mischievous grin,
Then laugh like the child he still is within.
He’ll fuss and he’ll bluster, he’ll drive you insane—
But deep down you know you’d not trade a grain.

For family’s messy, and love’s never neat,
It’s stubborn and loud, it’s sour and sweet.
And though he’s all backwards, both upside and down,
He built me a life, he gave me my crown.

So here’s to the dad who does all things awry,
Who talks too loud and won’t comply—
For love’s not tidy, it’s beautifully bound,
In everything done the wrong way ‘round.
