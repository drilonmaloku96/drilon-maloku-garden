A husk of a teenager was soullessly watching into the distance. His colleague a sixty year old man with rounded features.

Features that would indicate a softness when in an embrace, until the rugged, rough skin of his hands would touch you.

His eyes were just as soulless as his young companion’s. However, the difference lay in the niftiness of his bear-like hands. They were quick to lift the garbage bags- knot them up and throw it on the cart the young man pushed. His thin pale hands were veined and unhealthy. He moved his left hand sluggishly, almost hesitatingly, to his side to procure a little bit of cloth that seemed dirtier than the rim of the garbage cans in the train station- which the other man used to clean them with. His movements were a sequence of dashes that took half the time to complete compared to the readying from his young counterpart. 

It forced me to think of it as the perfect example for the cycle of acceptance. The boy clearly in first stage: denial, and the old man had already reached the last stage so long ago that he couldn’t remember. In this case, I feel compelled to change the name of the last stage of acceptance into “resignation”.

I passed by and sipped at my coffee. I tried to meet their eyes, but there was nothing to meet. Both of them focused their vision on empty space. Other than this similarity, both were at each-other’s opposite site of the spectrum. What a beautiful world!
