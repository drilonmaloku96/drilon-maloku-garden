I woke in the middle of the night, around two in the morning, still half-caught inside the dream I was having. In it, I found myself walking into a strange restaurant—one unlike anything I had ever seen. The walls had large openings built directly into them, almost like stable windows. Through these openings, the horses belonging to the guests could poke their heads into the dining room.
Each opening connected to a feeding trough on the outside, and on the inside the trough blended seamlessly into a dining table. Families sat together with their horses as if it were the most normal thing in the world—people and animals sharing a meal side by side.
At my own table, which had no such opening, I noticed a Chinese family seated nearby with their horse. The animal was gray and frail with tiny, tired eyes. It looked heartbreakingly old, barely managing to stand. I stared at it until curiosity got the better of me.
When the waiter passed by, I asked, “What’s the story with that horse over there?”
The waiter sighed. “Ah… that horse will be put down soon. This is the family’s last dinner together.”
The family continued eating quietly, while the horse chewed weakly beside them. When the meal ended, stable hands came to take the horse away. The moment it left, the Chinese family broke down completely. They cried uncontrollably—loud, aching sobs filling the restaurant. The old man at the head of the table didn’t even lift his face; he just let his tears fall straight down onto the wood, dripping and pooling beneath him.
Then the waiter returned—not with comfort, but with the check.
The old man glanced at the bill and instantly burst into even harder sobbing, as if the price itself were some unbearable final tragedy. Something about the combination—the heartbreak of losing the horse and the shock of the restaurant bill—hit me in such an absurd way that I couldn’t hold back. The whole situation became so darkly, ridiculously funny that laughter exploded out of me.
And that’s when I woke up.
